# Brookfield's Critical Lenses (1995)

**Framework:** Alternative reflection framework — assumption-focused
**Reference:** Brookfield, S. (1995). *Becoming a Critically Reflective Teacher*. Jossey-Bass.
**Used by:** `lesson/post_lesson_refl.md` (lesson cycle, term-level perspective) and `profession/term_reflection.md` (profession cycle, four lenses for critical reflection)

---

## Overview

Reflection through four critical lenses, each revealing different assumptions and blind spots. Best suited for deep, term-level reflection or peer observation debriefs — not for quick post-lesson reflection.

---

## The Four Lenses

### Lens 1: Autobiographical — Your own experience as a learner

**Prompt:**
```
"Hur påverkar dina egna erfarenheter som elev/student hur du undervisar detta ämne?"
```

Examines how the teacher's own learning history shapes their assumptions about what "good teaching" looks like.

### Lens 2: Students' Eyes — How students experience your teaching

**Prompt:**
```
"Om du kunde se lektionen genom elevernas ögon — vad skulle de säga fungerade och inte fungerade?"
```

If student feedback or assessment data is available, use it here. Otherwise, encourage the teacher to imagine the student perspective.

### Lens 3: Colleagues' Perspectives — How peers would view your practice

**Prompt:**
```
"Om en kollega observerade din lektion — vad tror du de skulle lägga märke till?"
```

Best used after actual peer observation. Can also be used hypothetically to surface assumptions.

### Lens 4: Theoretical Literature — What research says

**Prompt:**
```
"Finns det forskning som stöder eller utmanar ditt sätt att undervisa detta?"
```

Connect to the theoretical grounding in `lesson/post_lesson_refl.md` (Schön, Argyris) or `profession/term_reflection.md` (Klafki, Biesta, Korthagen). Invite the teacher to examine whether their practice aligns with established evidence.

---

## When to Suggest Brookfield

- End-of-term reflection (not quick post-lesson)
- After peer observation
- When the teacher feels "stuck" in their practice
- When assumptions need challenging ("jag har alltid gjort så här")
- Professional development conversations

## Differences from Gibbs

| Aspect | Gibbs | Brookfield |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Focus | What happened and why | What assumptions drive my practice |
| Depth | Lesson-level | Career/practice-level |
| Data needed | Plan, transcript, assessment | Primarily reflection and dialogue |
| Output | Specific changes to next plan | Awareness of assumptions and blind spots |
| Time required | 20–40 minutes | 45–90 minutes |
