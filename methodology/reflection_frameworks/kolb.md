# Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle (1984)

**Framework:** Alternative reflection framework — experimentation-focused
**Reference:** Kolb, D.A. (1984). *Experiential Learning: Experience as the Source of Learning and Development*. Prentice-Hall.
**Used by:** Background framing in v3.0 (referenced in `profession/term_reflection.md` as experiential learning context). Pragmatic keep — may become standalone tool (`src/tools/reflect_kolb.ts`).

---

## Overview

Four-phase cycle emphasising the transition from experience to experimentation. Best suited for teachers who learn by trying things — "what if I did it differently?" rather than "what went wrong?"

```
Concrete Experience (the lesson happened)
       ↓
Reflective Observation (what did I notice?)
       ↓
Abstract Conceptualisation (what principle explains this?)
       ↓
Active Experimentation (what will I try next time?)
       → loops back to Concrete Experience
```

---

## The Four Phases

### Phase 1: Concrete Experience

**Prompt:**
```
"Berätta om lektionen — vad hände, vad upplevde du?"
```

Combines Gibbs' steps 1 (Description) and 2 (Feelings) into a single holistic account. Less structured, more narrative.

### Phase 2: Reflective Observation

**Prompt:**
```
"Vad observerade du? Vad la du märke till hos eleverna?"
```

Focus on specific observations — what students did, said, asked. If transcript data is available, integrate here.

### Phase 3: Abstract Conceptualisation

**Prompt:**
```
"Finns det ett mönster eller en princip som förklarar vad du observerade?"
```

This is where pedagogical theory enters. Connect observations to frameworks (Hattie, Biggs, Demszky). The teacher generalises from the specific experience to a broader principle.

### Phase 4: Active Experimentation

**Prompt:**
```
"Vad vill du testa nästa gång? Vilken hypotes vill du pröva?"
```

Kolb frames improvement as experimentation rather than correction. The teacher is not "fixing mistakes" but "testing hypotheses." This framing suits teachers who are energised by innovation.

---

## When to Suggest Kolb

- Teacher says "jag vill prova något nytt"
- Teacher is more interested in experimentation than structured analysis
- The lesson was not problematic — the teacher wants to explore alternatives
- Creative, innovation-oriented teachers

## Differences from Gibbs

| Aspect | Gibbs | Kolb |
|--------|-------|------|
| Steps | 6 (structured) | 4 (fluid) |
| Feelings | Separate step | Integrated into experience |
| Theory | Applied during analysis | Applied during conceptualisation |
| Output | Change log with evidence | Hypothesis to test |
| Framing | "What went wrong and how to fix it" | "What if I tried this instead?" |
